Front Panel Indicator Configuration: 
ⓘ
In "Always Active" operation, the front panel indicators operate normally at all times. In this mode, the "Power Indicator Always Active" checkbox has no bearing on operation, and is hidden to minimize confusion.

The following two modes are affected by the "Power Indicator Always Active" checkbox:

In "Active on Alerts" operation, all the front panel indicators (except the power indicator if the checkbox is enabled) are off. The exception to this rule is when an alarm condition associated with any indicator exists, in which case all indicators show current status.

In "Off" operation, all front panel indicators (except the power indicator if the checkbox is enabled) are always off, regardless of alarm conditions.

Disclaimer: Be advised that no indications of alarm conditions will be displayed on the front panel in the "Off" mode of operation.

ⓘ
Link Aggregation is a means of obtaining a fatter pipe for transmission of data between the modem and another device configured in a compatible manner. Link Aggregation is also known as Ethernet Bonding, Port Bonding, NIC Bonding, and Port Trunking, among other things. This modem supports Link Aggregation Control Protocol (LACP), which is an IEEE standard defined in IEEE 802.3ad. It may be referred to as Dynamic or Mode 4 Link Aggregation by some equipment vendors. When Link Aggregation is enabled, a single logical link is created between the modem and the other networked device, which takes advantage of the bandwidth of two physical Ethernet ports, providing increased capacity and fault tolerance. Before enabling Link Aggregation, make sure the device you are connecting to the two local Ethernet ports also supports LACP.
